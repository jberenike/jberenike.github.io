About

I am a Digital Humanities generalist, striving to understand the potential as well as the constraints of the digital transformation of society and the Humanities. My main field is textual DH, with a PhD from VU University Amsterdam (‘Metaphor in Academic Discourse’).

Currently, I work as a postdoctoral researcher at the DHLab at the University of Basel (Switzerland).

My favorite textual features are metaphor, morpho-syntactic complexity, and modality. I plead happily guilty of having operationalized such constructs as "style", "narrative vividness", and "narrative coherence" for textual DH. We are now working on literary valuation ("Wertung"). My favorite literary epochs (using the term with fuzzy edges) are Modernism, Realism, and the Early Modern Period. I have been PI of the project "KOLIMO", building a representative corpus of literary Modernism in German, rendered in XML (eXist). Currently, I run most of my analyses in R.

In my research, I focus on the digital modeling of literary fictional discourse. One building block for this research are digitally represented literary artefacts that I analyse by means of mixed methods: manual annotation and text mining. My current main research questions are on valuation of literature by users of social reading platforms, the style of writers of the 19th and early 20th Centuries, as well as the representation of "nation", "cosmopolitanism", and "region". I am also keen on finding out more about the effects of reading on well-being.

To this end, I draw on Literary Historiography, Discourse Analysis, (Neo) Structuralism, and Cognitive Poetics.

Before moving to Basel, I was a postdoc (‘wissenschaftliche Mitarbeiterin’) at the Department of German Philology at Göttingen University, and postdoctoral member of the Courant Centre ‘Text structures’ and the Göttingen Centre for Digital Humanities (GCDH). After I obtained my MA (German Language and Literature, Sociology, and Spanish Language and Literature) at the University of Göttingen, including a year abroad at Universidad Autónoma de Madrid, I was a trainee at the Max-Planck-Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology Leipzig, Department of Developmental and Comparative Psychology.

I am PI of the Basel SNF-Digital Lives Project "Forschungslogiken in den texbasierten DH nach dem Machine Learning Turn", as well as Management Committee member of the COST Action Distant Reading for European Literary History and the current chair of the ADHO Special Interest Group "Digital Literary Stylistics".

Keywords: Digital Literary Stylistics, Methodology and Epistemology of Digital Humanities, Comparative Corpus Stylistics, Computational Discourse Analysis, Cultural Heritage, Metaphor Studies, Evaluation and Subjectivity on the Social Web Franz Kafka and Robert Musil.
